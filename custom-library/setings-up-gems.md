## Introduction: Why Build Custom AI Assistants?

A **Custom AI Assistant** is a pre-trained version of a large language model (LLM) that you personalize. Instead of typing the same long instructions every time (e.g., "Act as a Nigerian social media manager..."), you create a permanent assistant that **already knows who you are and what you need**.

The four major platforms that allow this are:
1. **Google Gemini** – calls them **Gems**.
2. **ChatGPT** – calls them **Custom GPTs**.
3. **Claude** – calls them **Projects**.
4. **DeepSeek** – uses **Custom Instructions/System Prompts**.

This guide will walk you through setting up your first Custom Assistant on each platform.

## Platform 1: Google Gemini – How to Create a Gem

**Best for:** Free users, students, and creators who want quick access.

**Step-by-Step Instructions:**

1. **Open Google Gemini:**
   - Go to `gemini.google.com`.
   - Sign in with your Google account.

2. **Navigate to Gems:**
   - On the left-hand sidebar, click on **"Gems"** (or the diamond icon).
   - If you don't see it, click the **"Explore Gems"** or **"Create a Gem"** button located near the top of the sidebar.

3. **Click "Create a Gem":**
   - You will be taken to the Gem Editor.

4. **Fill in the Gem Details:**
   - **Name:** Give your Gem a clear, descriptive name (e.g., "Nigerian Business Coach").
   - **Description:** (Optional) Write a short sentence explaining what it does (e.g., "Helps SMEs write proposals and marketing copy").

5. **Paste Your Custom Instructions:**
   - In the large text box labeled **"What should this Gem know?"**, paste your prompt.
   - *Example: "You are a friendly Student Success Coach. Explain complex topics using simple analogies. Help structure essays and study schedules."*

6. **Set the Tone (Optional):**
   - You can often select a "Style" (e.g., Professional, Creative, Casual).
   - For most business/education tasks, choose **"Professional"** or **"Clear"**.

7. **Save and Test:**
   - Click **"Save"** at the top right.
   - Once saved, click **"Start Chat"** or **"Test"** to try it out.

8. **Share Your Gem:**
   - Click the **"Share"** or **"Copy Link"** button.
   - Send this link to your students or team so they can use it instantly.

## Platform 2: ChatGPT – How to Create a Custom GPT

**Best for:** ChatGPT Plus (paid) users, advanced creators, and those who want to upload knowledge files (PDFs, text files, data).

**Step-by-Step Instructions:**

1. **Open ChatGPT:**
   - Go to `chat.openai.com`.
   - Log in to your ChatGPT Plus account.

2. **Navigate to GPT Builder:**
   - Click on **"Explore GPTs"** (located in the left-hand sidebar, usually under the ChatGPT logo).
   - Click the **"Create a GPT"** or **"Create"** button in the top-right corner.

3. **Switch to Manual Mode (Recommended):**
   - By default, the "Create" tab uses a conversation to build the GPT.
   - Click the **"Configure"** tab next to it for full control.

4. **Fill in the Basic Information:**
   - **Name:** Enter a clear name (e.g., "Viral Content Creator").
   - **Description:** What does this GPT do? (e.g., "Generates TikTok hooks and Instagram captions for Nigerian creators").

5. **Enter Your Instructions:**
   - In the **"Instructions"** box, paste your prompt.
   - *Example: "You are a Viral Content Strategist. Generate 5 creative video hooks, write engaging captions with Nigerian hashtags, and suggest a content calendar."*

6. **Upload Knowledge Files (Optional but Powerful):**
   - Under **"Knowledge"**, click **"Upload files"**.
   - You can upload PDFs, Word docs, or text files (e.g., a list of past successful posts, your pricing list, or your brand voice guide).

7. **Configure Capabilities (Optional):**
   - Tick **"Web Browsing"**, **"DALL-E Image Generation"**, or **"Code Interpreter"** if your GPT needs these features.

8. **Test Your GPT:**
   - Use the **Preview** window on the right side to type in a prompt and test your GPT.

9. **Save and Publish:**
   - Click **"Update"** or **"Save"**.
   - Select **"Only me"** (private), **"Anyone with the link"** (shareable), or **"Public"**.
   - Click **"Confirm"**.
   - Copy the generated link to share it with your community.

## Platform 3: Claude – How to Create a Project

**Best for:** Researchers, academic writers, and developers who need long-context memory and dedicated workspaces.

**Step-by-Step Instructions:**

1. **Open Claude:**
   - Go to `claude.ai`.
   - Log in to your account (Claude Pro recommended for Projects).

2. **Navigate to Projects:**
   - Look at the left sidebar. Click on the **"Projects"** tab (it usually looks like a folder icon).

3. **Create a New Project:**
   - Click the **"Create Project"** button.

4. **Name and Description:**
   - **Title:** Give your project a clear name (e.g., "Research Paper Assistant").
   - **Description:** Add a brief note about the project's purpose.

5. **Paste Project Instructions:**
   - In the **"Instructions"** or **"System Prompt"** box, paste your guidelines.
   - *Example: "You are a Research Assistant. Help me structure academic papers, find counter-arguments, and suggest case studies for my Nigerian context."*

6. **Upload Project Knowledge (Best Feature):**
   - Use the **"Knowledge"** section to upload files (PDFs, TXT, etc.).
   - This allows Claude to read and reference your specific documents (e.g., your thesis, company policies, or research articles).

7. **Set a Custom Chat Title (Optional):**
   - You can change the title of the chat inside the Project later.

8. **Start Using Your Project:**
   - Click **"Create Project"**.
   - Claude will now remember your instructions and knowledge files for every conversation inside this project.

9. **Share Your Project (Claude Team/Enterprise only):**
   - For sharing with students, you can either copy your "System Prompt" text and send it to them, or use the Project's "Share" function if you have the Team version.

## Platform 4: DeepSeek AI – How to Set Up Custom Instructions

**Best for:** Users who want a high-quality free alternative to ChatGPT. DeepSeek handles long contexts extremely well.

**Note:** DeepSeek does not yet have a built-in "Project/Gem" folder system. Instead, you use its **"System Prompt"** feature.

**Step-by-Step Instructions:**

1. **Open DeepSeek AI:**
   - Go to `chat.deepseek.com` or open the DeepSeek mobile app.
   - Create a free account.

2. **Start a New Chat:**
   - Click **"New Chat"** to start a fresh conversation.

3. **Set Your System Prompt (The Workaround):**
   - There is no dedicated "Custom Instructions" settings menu on the web version. You must paste your instructions at the **very beginning** of your conversation.
